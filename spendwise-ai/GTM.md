# Go-To-Market

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering manager or CTO at a 10–50 person seed or Series A startup. Has 3–15 engineers, all using at least two AI tools, paying for them on a company card with no systematic review process. Doesn't know the total AI tool bill off the top of their head. Feels vaguely that it's getting out of hand but hasn't had 30 minutes to audit it.

**Secondary:** Founder-engineer running a 2–5 person product team where they're also the one paying the bills. Uses ChatGPT and Cursor personally, added Claude when it got better at code, subscribed to Gemini during a free trial and forgot to cancel.

Both users are cost-conscious right now specifically — the 2026 macro environment has pushed even well-funded teams to scrutinise burn more carefully.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days, $0 Budget

**Week 1 — Seeding with proof:**
Post a Show HN with a real audit example: "I audited our team's AI tools and found we were paying for overlapping tools. Built a tool to do this for any team." Show HN works when you lead with a specific, surprising finding rather than a product description. Target 30–50 upvotes → 200–400 visitors → 20–40 audits.

**Week 2 — Targeted community drops:**
Post the tool in Indie Hackers ("I built a free AI spend auditor — here's what I found about my own stack"). IH readers are the exact demographic. Also post in r/ExperiencedDevs framed as "I got tired of not knowing our total AI spend." Do not post as a promo — post as a finding, tool as the solution.

**Week 3 — Twitter/X thread:**
Write a thread: "I audited 50 startups' AI tool spending. Here's what I found." Use anonymised aggregate data from early audits. Lead with the most surprising finding (e.g. "37% of teams are paying for both ChatGPT Team and Claude Team for the same use case"). End with the tool link. Target: one engineer manager with >10k followers quotes it.

**Week 4 — Direct outreach:**
DM 20 engineering managers on X who have publicly complained about tool costs or shared their stack. Not a cold pitch — "I built something that might be relevant to what you posted last week." Conversion target: 5 of 20 run an audit, 2 share it.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that overspent on AI infrastructure — that's how they source discounted credits. Those same companies have engineers who are *currently* looking for ways to reduce AI spend. Credex can reach out to their existing network and offer the audit tool as a free resource. This is warm outreach to pre-qualified users that no competitor can replicate.

Additionally: any Credex consultation that results in a credit purchase becomes a case study. "Company X ran the audit, found $8,000/year in waste, bought Credex credits for $12,000, net positive ROI in month 1." One published case study shared by the customer is worth more than any paid acquisition.
